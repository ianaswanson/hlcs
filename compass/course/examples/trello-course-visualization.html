Trello Course Roadmap - Q2 2011

VISION (Reference from Destination)

Work should be visible. When you can see everything laid out in front of you - what's done, what's happening, what's next - collaboration stops feeling like coordination overhead and starts feeling like flow. We believe the simple act of moving a card from 'Doing' to 'Done' should feel satisfying, that teams deserve tools that fade into the background and let the work shine through. We're building the whiteboard that travels with you, the sticky note that never falls off, the shared brain for any team doing anything.

STRATEGY (2-3 Years)

Diagnosis: Project management tools have failed small teams not because of missing features, but because of forced complexity. Teams abandon Basecamp, Jira, and MS Project not because these tools can't do what they need, but because the cognitive overhead of using them exceeds the value of tracking the work. The 30-second learning curve is not a nice-to-have - it's the entire problem we're solving.

Guiding Principles:

• When choosing between features and simplicity, we choose simplicity - every new capability must not increase cognitive load for first-time users

• We'll know we're successful when people teach Trello to colleagues in under 60 seconds without documentation

• Our approach to product decisions: if it requires explaining in the UI, we haven't designed it simply enough

• Word-of-mouth growth over paid acquisition - the product experience is our marketing

Focus Areas:

• Core board/list/card interaction - make drag-and-drop feel effortless and immediate

• Real-time collaboration - multiple people on the same board must feel like magic

• Zero-friction onboarding - from landing page to productive use in under 2 minutes

• Freemium conversion discovery - understand what power users actually need and will pay for

Tradeoffs:

• We will not build Gantt charts, time tracking, or resource management - that's enterprise PM territory we're avoiding

• We will not add workflow automation or complex rules - teams should adapt Trello to their process, not configure Trello

• We will defer mobile apps until web product is proven - focus beats breadth in year one

• We will not pursue enterprise sales - small teams are our beachhead, enterprise comes later
